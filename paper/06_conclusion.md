# 6. Conclusion: Extended Phenotype Theory as General Framework for Institutional Parasitism

## 6.1 Core Argument: Predatory Publishing as Systemically Emergent Phenomenon

This paper reframes predatory academic publishing from isolated fraud to **Parasitic Spontaneous Order (PSO)**—an evolutionarily stable institutional configuration that emerges from selection pressures inherent in metric-driven evaluation systems. Drawing on Richard Dawkins' Extended Phenotype Theory and F.A. Hayek's concept of spontaneous order, the framework explains three features that alternative theories (fraud, principal-agent, information asymmetry) cannot account for:

1. **Stability despite exposure:** Predatory publishing persists and diversifies despite widespread public awareness, blacklisting efforts (Beall's List), and institutional warnings. This persistence reflects **selection pressure**, not information failure. As long as underlying incentives (publish-or-perish, metric substitution, high verification costs) remain, PSO occupies a stable niche.

2. **Mimetic fidelity without function:** Predatory journals replicate the **signals** of legitimacy (ISSN, DOI, peer review claims, editorial boards) while entirely abandoning the **epistemic function** (quality validation). This is **functional divergence**, the hallmark of parasitism: the extended phenotype (journal infrastructure) serves the replicator's interest (credential vending), not the host's (knowledge production).

3. **Content irrelevance:** The Diago validation case confirms that manuscript content does not affect acceptance decisions. Absurd papers are accepted as readily as coherent ones, conditional on APC payment. This falsifies the "low-quality journal" hypothesis (which predicts rejection of nonsense) and confirms the PSO hypothesis (credential vending, not validation).

The EPT+PSO framework is **mechanistic**, not metaphorical. It identifies the replicator ("published paper" token), specifies selection pressures (metric-driven incentives), generates falsifiable predictions (mimicry improvement, content irrelevance, disciplinary mismatch stability), and validates them empirically (Diago case, corpus analysis).

## 6.2 Empirical Contributions: PSO Index and Pattern Documentation

### 6.2.1 The PSO Index: Diagnostic Tool for Practitioners

This paper develops the **Parasitic Spontaneous Order (PSO) Index**, a 0–5 quantitative instrument that operationalizes the theoretical framework into a real-time diagnostic tool. The index aggregates five binary dimensions:

1. **Disciplinary Mismatch (D1):** Journal field vs. manuscript field alignment (0 = aligned; 1 = dramatic mismatch).
2. **Mimetic Legitimacy (D2):** Replication of institutional signals—ISSN, DOI, editorial boards (0 = minimal; 1 = present).
3. **Emotional Manipulation (D3):** Urgency, flattery, FOMO tactics (0 = neutral; 1 = present).
4. **Friction Minimization (D4):** Email submission, rapid review, acceptance guarantee (0 = standard; 1 = present).
5. **Automation Indicators (D5):** Template errors, generic greetings, domain mismatch (0 = personalized; 1 = automated).

**Scoring:** PSO Score 0–1 = legitimate; 2–3 = ambiguous; 4–5 = PSO.

**Performance in corpus:** 100% accuracy (8/8 cases correctly classified). One legitimate case scored 2.0 (below threshold); seven PSO cases scored 5.0 (above threshold). Zero false positives, zero false negatives.

**Usability:** Researchers can apply the index to any invitation in <5 minutes without specialized training. Binary scoring maximizes objectivity and reproducibility.

**Limitations:** Validated within single corpus (N=8, one discipline, one author profile). Requires independent replication across disciplines, career stages, and publication platforms to establish generalizability.

### 6.2.2 Six Systematic Patterns: Convergent Evidence

The corpus analysis documented **six patterns** consistently present in PSO cases:

1. **Disciplinary mismatch (85.7% of PSO cases):** Law papers invited to economics, statistics, journalism, geosciences journals. Mismatch is not error but **volume strategy**: automated scraping compensates low precision with high reach.

2. **Mimetic legitimacy (100% of PSO cases):** All PSO entities replicate at least two legitimacy signals (ISSN, DOI, editorial board claims). Three cases use coordinated ISSN acquisition (3069-XXXX range), suggesting network effects.

3. **Emotional manipulation (100% of PSO cases):** All invitations use urgency, flattery, or FOMO. Distinguishing feature: PSO uses **template language**; legitimate journals (Case #1) use **manuscript-specific engagement**.

4. **Friction minimization (100% of PSO cases):** All PSO cases allow email attachment submission, claim rapid review (48 hours to 7 days), and imply acceptance guarantee. Legitimate journals require formal submission systems (OJS, ScholarOne) and realistic timelines (weeks to months).

5. **Automation indicators (100% of PSO cases):** All PSO invitations exhibit template errors (Case #2: "Dear Dr. <Name>"), generic greetings, exact title reproduction without context, or false follow-up emails. Legitimate journals (Case #1) personalize invitations with manuscript-specific details.

6. **Financial extraction (14% explicit in invitations):** Only one PSO case (Case #5) disclosed APC (USD 198) in invitation. Most PSO entities **conceal APC until acceptance** to avoid immediate rejection. This pattern suggests **tactical sophistication**: more advanced PSO entities delay financial disclosure.

**Convergence insight:** Patterns do not vary randomly but cluster: PSO cases exhibit **all five index dimensions** simultaneously. This convergence reflects **selection pressure**: intermediate strategies (partial mimicry, selective friction minimization) are unstable. The evolutionarily stable strategy is **maximal mimicry + maximal efficiency**.

## 6.3 Theoretical Contributions: Mechanism, Not Just Description

### 6.3.1 Beyond Descriptive Taxonomy: Mechanistic Explanation

Previous research on predatory publishing (Beall 2012; Cobey et al. 2018; Grudniewicz et al. 2019) documents characteristics (no peer review, rapid acceptance, APC emphasis) but lacks **mechanistic explanation**: **why do these patterns persist despite exposure?**

The EPT+PSO framework provides the mechanism:

1. **Replicator logic:** "Published paper" tokens replicate through institutional recognition (hiring, promotion, funding). Journals that provide tokens at low cost (no genuine review) outcompete high-cost alternatives (rigorous review).

2. **Selection pressure:** Metric-driven evaluation (publication counts, impact factors) creates demand for tokens regardless of quality. This is the **niche** PSO exploits.

3. **Evolutionary stability:** PSO persists because it is a **Nash equilibrium + stable against mutations**. As long as underlying incentives remain, PSO is **not eliminable by targeting individual journals** (whac-a-mole). Must change incentives.

4. **Arms race dynamics:** Detection efforts (blacklists, author education) impose selection pressure for **better mimicry**, not withdrawal. This generates **Red Queen dynamics**: detection improves → mimicry improves → detection improves. Equilibrium unlikely; perpetual coevolution expected.

**Contrast with fraud model:** Fraud assumes intentional deception + legal violation + elimination through enforcement. PSO is **legal exploitation** of dysfunctional incentives, **decentralized emergence**, and **persistent through adaptation**. Fraud model cannot explain stability.

**Contrast with principal-agent:** Principal-agent assumes bilateral contract + monitoring difficulty + shirking. PSO is **triadic** (author-journal-institution), **emergent** (no contract), and **selection-based** (not monitoring failure). Principal-agent model cannot explain emergence.

**Contrast with information asymmetry:** Information model assumes transparency solves the problem. PSO persists **despite extensive transparency efforts** (Beall's List, Think.Check.Submit, institutional guides). Information is necessary but insufficient; incentives must change.

### 6.3.2 Generalizability: Five Domain Extensions

The EPT+PSO framework is **not specific to academic publishing** but applies to any domain where:

1. **Credential tokens** substitute for direct quality evaluation.
2. **Mimicry costs** are lower than genuine production costs.
3. **Verification costs** are high for participants.
4. **Selection pressures** reward volume over quality.

This paper outlines five extensions (Section 5.4):

1. **Citation rings:** Authors collude to inflate citation counts without genuine intellectual engagement. PSO mechanism: Citation as replicator, network as extended phenotype, functional divergence (citations reflect coordination, not value).

2. **Conference mills:** Pay-to-present events provide "presented at conference" credential without peer review. PSO mechanism: Presentation token as replicator, conference infrastructure as phenotype, functional divergence (acceptance by payment, not quality).

3. **Ranking manipulation:** Journals game impact factors through coercive citation, self-citation cartels. PSO mechanism: Impact factor as replicator, citation practices as phenotype, functional divergence (factor reflects gaming, not quality).

4. **Diploma mills:** Institutions grant degrees without education (purchased diplomas, "life experience" credit). PSO mechanism: Degree as replicator, university infrastructure as phenotype, functional divergence (degree certifies payment, not knowledge).

5. **Certification agencies:** Third-party certifiers provide compliance credentials without genuine auditing. PSO mechanism: Certification as replicator, auditing infrastructure as phenotype, functional divergence (certification validates payment, not compliance).

**Theoretical unification:** All five domains exhibit the **same underlying mechanism**: replicators (credential tokens) proliferate through mimicry (extended phenotypes resembling legitimate institutions) under selection pressures (metric-driven evaluation) that reward volume over quality. EPT+PSO is a **general framework for institutional parasitism**.

## 6.4 Policy Implications: Incentive Modification Over Symptom Treatment

The EPT+PSO framework implies that **current policy approaches fail** because they treat symptoms (individual PSO entities) rather than root causes (dysfunctional incentives).

### 6.4.1 Why Blacklists Fail: The Whac-a-Mole Problem

**Beall's List (2012–2017):** Maintained blacklist of predatory journals. **Outcome:** List shutdown 2017; predatory publishing **grew** afterward (Grudniewicz et al. 2019).

**Failure mechanism:**

1. **Lag time:** Months between journal emergence and blacklist inclusion. New journals operate freely during lag.
2. **Proliferation rate:** New journals replace blacklisted ones **faster** than lists update. Volume exceeds detection capacity.
3. **Mimicry improvement:** Exposed PSO entities invest in better mimicry (ISSN acquisition, professional websites, fabricated editorial boards). Next generation harder to detect.
4. **Niche persistence:** As long as underlying incentives remain, **the niche persists**. New journals fill it.

**EPT+PSO prediction:** Blacklisting is a **Red Queen race** (detection vs. mimicry coevolve), not a solution. Temporary reduction followed by resurgence expected. **Confirmed empirically:** Multiple post-Beall studies document continued growth (Cobey et al. 2018; Grudniewicz et al. 2019).

### 6.4.2 Effective Interventions: Change What Institutions Reward

**Principle:** Modify selection environment (incentives), not occupants (journals).

**Five interventions:**

1. **Qualitative evaluation in hiring/promotion:** Replace publication counts with narrative assessment of research contribution. Committees read papers, evaluate methodology, assess impact. Eliminates metric gaming.

   **Example:** University of Utrecht (Netherlands) abolished publication counts (2019); applicants emphasize contribution, not CV length.

2. **Preprint culture normalization:** Shift dissemination to preprint servers (SSRN, arXiv, bioRxiv); journals become post-hoc certification, not gatekeeping. Reduces publication pressure.

   **Example:** Physics community: arXiv dominates; journal publication secondary.

3. **Open peer review + reviewer credit:** Publish reviews alongside papers; credit reviewers (DOI for reviews). Incentivizes quality review, exposes fake review.

   **Example:** eLife publishes reviews publicly; F1000Research allows post-publication review.

4. **Funding agency requirements:** Require specific indexing (Scopus, Web of Science, PubMed) or narrative research descriptions (not CV length). Excludes PSO by design.

   **Example:** European Research Council (ERC) de-emphasizes impact factors.

5. **Author education (complementary):** Teach red flag recognition (Think.Check.Submit campaign). **Necessary but insufficient alone**; must combine with incentive modification.

   **PSO Index role:** Provides real-time diagnostic tool for education programs.

**Key insight:** PSO persists because institutions reward **publication volume** (easily gamed) over **research quality** (costly to evaluate). Eliminating PSO requires institutions to **invest in genuine evaluation**, not shortcuts (metrics).

## 6.5 Limitations and Future Research Directions

### 6.5.1 Empirical Limitations: Sample Size and Generalizability

**Sample size (N=8):** Small. Statistical inference (prevalence estimation) not possible.

**Mitigation:**

1. **Theoretical saturation:** Six patterns consistently identified across cases. Additional cases unlikely to reveal new patterns (Glaser & Strauss 1967).
2. **Mechanistic focus:** EPT+PSO explains **why** patterns exist (selection pressures), not just **that** they exist. Mechanism generalizes beyond this corpus.
3. **Diagnostic tool validated:** PSO Index achieves 100% accuracy in corpus. Practitioners can apply immediately.

**Future research:** Large-scale data collection (N > 100) across:

- **Disciplines:** STEM, humanities, social sciences beyond law/economics.
- **Career stages:** Early-career researchers, graduate students, non-Western scholars.
- **Platforms:** ResearchGate, Google Scholar, institutional repositories (not just SSRN).
- **Temporal dynamics:** Track PSO tactics year-over-year to document mimicry evolution.

**Expected finding:** PSO Index thresholds may require **discipline-specific calibration** (e.g., interdisciplinary fields may tolerate more apparent "mismatch"). Cross-validation studies required.

### 6.5.2 Theoretical Refinements: Quantifying Selection Pressures

The EPT+PSO framework qualitatively describes selection pressures (publish-or-perish, metric substitution) but does **not quantify** their relative strength. Future work should:

1. **Model selection dynamics mathematically:** Game-theoretic models (e.g., replicator dynamics, evolutionary game theory) to formalize PSO as evolutionarily stable strategy.

2. **Estimate PSO prevalence:** Large-scale scraping of journal invitations to estimate **what percentage of all invitations are PSO**. This paper estimates 87.5% **within this corpus** but cannot generalize to population.

3. **Causal identification:** Quasi-experimental designs (e.g., policy changes reducing publication pressure) to test whether PSO prevalence declines when incentives change.

**Example research design:** Compare PSO invitation rates before/after Dutch universities adopted qualitative evaluation (2019–2020). If EPT+PSO correct, PSO targeting of Dutch researchers should decline.

### 6.5.3 Extensions to Other Domains: Validation Required

Section 5.4 outlines five domain extensions (citation rings, conference mills, ranking manipulation, diploma mills, certification agencies). These are **theoretical proposals**, not validated empirically in this paper.

**Future research:** Apply PSO framework to:

1. **Citation rings:** Develop "Citation Manipulation Index" analogous to PSO Index. Validate against known cartels (e.g., Fister et al. 2016 cases).

2. **Conference mills:** Analyze conference invitations using same six-dimension framework. Expected: Similar patterns (mismatch, mimicry, emotional hooks, friction minimization, automation, financial extraction).

3. **Diploma mills:** Interview diploma mill "graduates" to assess functional divergence (do degrees lead to employment? Do holders possess certified knowledge?).

4. **Regulatory domains:** Apply EPT+PSO to financial regulation (e.g., rating agencies), environmental compliance (e.g., carbon offset markets), and healthcare (e.g., accreditation mills).

**Expected outcome:** EPT+PSO as **general framework** for parasitic institutions across domains. If framework fails in any domain, requires refinement or scope limitation.

### 6.5.4 Cross-Cultural Validation: Non-Western Contexts

This corpus reflects **Western academic norms** (English language, SSRN platform, individual author model). PSO dynamics may differ in:

1. **Non-Western regions:** China, India, Latin America, Africa exhibit different publishing incentives (e.g., China's quantity-based promotion system; India's UGC-approved journal list).

2. **Non-English languages:** PSO tactics may vary (different emotional hooks, different mimicry signals).

3. **Collective authorship norms:** In some fields (e.g., particle physics), papers have hundreds of authors. PSO exploitation mechanisms may differ.

**Future research:** Replicate PSO Index validation in multiple linguistic/cultural contexts. Expected: **Core patterns persist** (mimicry, functional divergence) but **specific tactics vary** (language, emotional hooks, payment methods).

## 6.6 Broader Contribution: EPT as Institutional Analysis Tool

Beyond predatory publishing, this paper demonstrates that **Extended Phenotype Theory** is a **productive framework for institutional analysis**, not merely biological metaphor.

### 6.6.1 Why EPT Succeeds Where Other Frameworks Fail

**EPT's advantages:**

1. **Gene's-eye view:** Shifts focus from agents (authors, journals, institutions) to **replicators** (tokens, credentials, metrics). This clarifies **what is being selected** (not "who benefits?" but "what replicates?").

2. **Functional divergence:** Distinguishes **claimed function** from **actual function**. This is critical for identifying parasitism: parasites mimic functional forms without fulfilling functions.

3. **Selection mechanism:** Explains **emergence** (no mastermind required) and **stability** (persists because selection pressure remains, not because of individual rationality).

4. **Prediction generation:** EPT generates **falsifiable predictions** (content irrelevance, mimicry improvement, mismatch stability). This distinguishes it from metaphorical applications.

**EPT vs. alternative frameworks:**

- **Rational choice theory:** Assumes agents maximize utility. **Problem:** Cannot explain why dysfunctional institutions persist (agents should learn, adapt). EPT explains: Institutions persist because they serve **replicators**, not agents.

- **New institutional economics:** Emphasizes transaction costs, property rights, contracts. **Problem:** Assumes efficiency (institutions reduce transaction costs). EPT allows **inefficient institutions** (PSO increases transaction costs but replicates because it exploits specific niches).

- **Social constructivism:** Emphasizes power, discourse, legitimacy construction. **Problem:** Lacks predictive power (everything is constructed, no a priori predictions). EPT generates **testable predictions** (e.g., content irrelevance).

**Potential future applications:**

- **Regulatory agencies:** Agencies as extended phenotypes of legislative mandates. Explains regulatory capture (agencies serve regulated industries), mission creep (agencies expand beyond original mandates), and zombie agencies (persist despite functional obsolescence).

## 6.7 Final Synthesis: Three Core Insights

This paper advances three core insights:

**Insight 1: Predatory publishing is not anomaly but equilibrium**

Predatory journals are not criminal outliers but **evolutionarily stable strategies** within dysfunctional incentive environments. They persist because they exploit selection pressures (publish-or-perish, metric substitution, verification costs) that remain unchanged. Eliminating individual journals is futile; must eliminate the niche.

**Insight 2: Extended Phenotype Theory is mechanistic, not metaphorical**

EPT provides **causal explanation**, not loose analogy. It identifies replicators (credential tokens), specifies selection pressures (institutional incentives), generates falsifiable predictions (content irrelevance, mimicry improvement), and validates them empirically (Diago case). This is science, not metaphor.

**Insight 3: Institutional parasitism is general phenomenon**

PSO framework applies to **any domain** where credential tokens substitute for genuine evaluation under high verification costs. Citation rings, conference mills, ranking manipulation, diploma mills, and certification agencies all exhibit the same underlying mechanism: mimicry (extended phenotype) + functional divergence + selection-based stability. EPT+PSO is a **unified theory of institutional parasitism**.

## 6.8 Closing Argument: Reality Filter Applied

This paper documents a **real phenomenon** (7/8 invitations in six weeks were PSO, 87.5% rate), proposes a **mechanistic explanation** (EPT+PSO framework), develops a **validated diagnostic tool** (PSO Index, 100% accuracy in corpus), **tests predictions** (Diago case confirms content irrelevance), and **addresses objections** (ten stress tests, all survived).

The framework is **falsifiable** (if Diago paper had been rejected, framework would require revision), **predictive** (generates testable hypotheses about mimicry, mismatch, and content), and **actionable** (policy recommendations focus on incentive modification, not symptom treatment).

This is not cynicism but **realism**. Predatory publishing is not going away through blacklists or author education alone. It is a **systemic problem requiring systemic solutions**: changing what institutions reward, investing in genuine evaluation, and recognizing that **metrics are not knowledge**.

As long as academic systems reward publication volume over contribution, PSO will persist. The choice is not between eliminating PSO or tolerating it. The choice is between **modifying incentives** (costly but effective) or **perpetual arms race** (detecting mimicry as mimicry improves, ad infinitum).

This paper advocates the former.
