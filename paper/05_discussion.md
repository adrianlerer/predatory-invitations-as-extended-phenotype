# 5. Discussion: Interpreting Parasitic Spontaneous Order Through Extended Phenotype Theory

## 5.1 Core Thesis: Predatory Publishing as Evolutionarily Stable Strategy

The empirical findings (Section 4) confirm the theoretical predictions (Section 2): predatory journals exhibit **convergent patterns** (100% of PSO cases scored 5/5 across six dimensions) not because of centralized coordination but because of **selection pressures** inherent in metric-driven academic systems. This section interprets findings mechanistically through the EPT+PSO framework and addresses broader implications.

### 5.1.1 Why 100% Perfect Scores? The Convergence Puzzle

All seven PSO cases scored **5.0/5.0** on the index—no variation, no intermediate strategies. This is not sampling artifact (N=8 is small but patterns are stark) but reflects **evolutionary logic**:

**Intermediate strategies are unstable:**

- **Partial mimicry (score 2–3):** Insufficient legitimacy signals to attract discerning authors; insufficient resource extraction to sustain operations. Journals attempting this strategy either (a) invest in genuine quality and become legitimate or (b) abandon quality and converge on PSO.

- **Obvious scams (score 5 but poorly executed):** High PSO score but crude execution (no website, random email domains) fail immediately. Selection eliminates them.

- **Refined PSO (score 5, well executed):** All six dimensions present, mimicry sophisticated enough to pass superficial inspection. This is the **evolutionarily stable strategy**: optimal balance between mimicry cost and resource extraction.

**Analogy:** Batesian mimicry in biology. Harmless hoverflies mimic wasps (black-yellow stripes). Partial mimicry (only black, or only yellow) fails to deter predators. Perfect mimicry succeeds. Similarly, PSO journals must replicate **all five signals** (mismatch tolerated because volume compensates, but other signals mandatory) to attract submissions.

### 5.1.2 The One Legitimate Case: JCLLT as Negative Control

Case #1 (JCLLT) scored **2.0**—below the PSO threshold (4.0) but not zero. This is **theoretically expected**:

1. **Legitimate journals must signal credibility** (DOI, editorial boards, professional infrastructure). These overlap with PSO mimicry, but **quality differs**: JCLLT's editorial board is verifiable; PSO boards are fabricated.

2. **Enthusiasm is editorially appropriate:** JCLLT expressed interest **specific to manuscript content**, while PSO cases use **generic templates**.

**Key distinction:** JCLLT exhibits **form with function**; PSO exhibits **form without function**. The PSO Index does not measure **presence of signals** (which would misclassify all journals) but **functional divergence** (signals present + other red flags).

**Implication:** The index is **robust** to false positives. New journals can score low (legitimate) despite using some mimicry signals, as long as functional integrity is present.

## 5.2 EPT Mechanisms: Three Validated Predictions

### 5.2.1 Prediction 1: Mimetic Fidelity Increases Over Time

**Prediction:** As detection efforts improve, PSO entities invest in better mimicry.

**Evidence (preliminary):**

- **Early PSO (2012–2017, Beall's List era):** Crude tactics (Gmail addresses, no ISSN, obvious template errors).
- **Current PSO (2024–2025, this corpus):** Sophisticated tactics (ISSN acquisition, professional websites, fabricated editorial boards using real researcher names without consent).

**Case #6, #7, #8 (3069-XXXX ISSN pattern):** Suggests **coordinated acquisition** of ISSN numbers from a single source, indicating:

1. **Professionalization:** PSO entities now know ISSN is a critical legitimacy signal.
2. **Network effects:** Multiple journals operated by same entity or consortium, sharing mimicry infrastructure.
3. **Adaptation to blacklists:** As individual journals are exposed, new journals emerge with **better mimicry**.

**Longitudinal test (future research):** Track PSO tactics year-over-year. EPT+PSO predicts **arms race dynamics**: detection improves → mimicry improves → detection improves. Stable equilibrium unlikely; perpetual Red Queen race expected.

### 5.2.2 Prediction 2: Content Irrelevance (Diago Case Validation)

**Prediction:** PSO journals accept papers regardless of content quality because their function is credential vending, not epistemic validation.

**Diago case (Appendix A) summary:**

- **Researcher:** Submitted intentionally absurd paper (mathematical errors, nonsensical claims, contradictory arguments) to a journal scoring 5/5 on PSO Index.
- **Hypothesis:** If journal functions as credential vendor (PSO), paper accepted without substantive review.
- **Result:** Paper accepted within **48 hours** with **formatting requests only** (no substantive feedback). APC requested (USD 150).
- **Interpretation:** Confirms functional divergence. Journal's **claimed function** (peer review) absent; **actual function** (credential vending) confirmed.

**Significance:**

This is the **strongest evidence** for EPT+PSO framework. Alternative explanations fail:

- **Low-quality model:** Predatory journals are simply bad at peer review. **Problem:** Even bad review rejects nonsense. Diago paper was **absurd**, not merely weak.
  
- **Fraud model:** Journals intentionally deceive. **Problem:** Doesn't explain **stability** (why fraud persists despite exposure).

- **Information asymmetry:** Authors don't know journal is predatory. **Problem:** Diago **knew** (deliberately tested hypothesis). Journal accepted anyway.

**EPT+PSO uniquely predicts:** Content irrelevant. Journal replicates because it provides **credential tokens**, not knowledge. Diago case confirms.

### 5.2.3 Prediction 3: Disciplinary Mismatch Stability

**Prediction:** PSO journals scrape indiscriminately; mismatch persists because volume compensates for low precision.

**Evidence:** 6/7 PSO cases (85.7%) exhibit **dramatic mismatch** (law paper to economics, statistics, journalism, geosciences).

**Why mismatch is stable:**

1. **Volume strategy:** Send 10,000 invitations. Even 0.5% acceptance (50 submissions) sustains operations if APC = USD 200 (revenue = USD 10,000).

2. **Desperation exploitation:** A small fraction of authors (under tenure threat, desperate for publications) accept mismatched invitations. PSO optimizes for **desperation**, not discernment.

3. **No penalty for rejection:** Sending mismatched invitations costs near-zero (automated). Rejections don't harm reputation (no reputation to protect).

**Control contrast:** Case #1 (JCLLT) exhibits **perfect alignment**. Legitimate journals cannot tolerate mismatch (damages editorial reputation, wastes reviewer time). PSO journals face no such constraint.

**Implication:** Mismatch is not **error** but **strategy**. It distinguishes PSO from low-quality legitimate journals.

## 5.3 Policy Implications: Systemic Solutions vs. Symptom Treatment

### 5.3.1 Why Blacklists Fail: The Whac-a-Mole Problem

**Beall's List (2012–2017):** Maintained a blacklist of predatory journals. **Outcome:** List shutdown in 2017; predatory publishing **grew** afterward.

**Why blacklists fail:**

1. **Lag time:** Months between journal emergence and blacklist inclusion. New journals operate freely during lag.

2. **Journal proliferation:** New journals replace blacklisted ones faster than lists update. PSO entities simply rebrand.

3. **Mimicry improvement:** Exposed PSO entities invest in better mimicry (ISSN, professional websites, fabricated boards). Next generation harder to detect.

4. **No root cause address:** Blacklists eliminate **symptoms** (individual journals) but not **disease** (dysfunctional incentives). The niche remains; new occupants fill it.

**EPT+PSO framework explains:** As long as **selection pressures** (publish-or-perish, metric substitution, high verification costs) remain, PSO persists. Eliminating journals is futile; must eliminate **the niche**.

### 5.3.2 Effective Interventions: Modify Selection Environment

**Principle:** Change what institutions reward, not what journals exist.

**Intervention 1: Qualitative Evaluation in Hiring/Promotion**

- **Current system:** Count publications; use journal impact factor as proxy for quality.
- **Problem:** Incentivizes volume over substance; creates PSO niche.
- **Solution:** Require **narrative evaluation** of research impact. Committees read papers, assess contribution, engage with methodology. Slow, costly, but eliminates metric gaming.

**Example:** University of Utrecht (Netherlands) abolished publication counts in hiring (2019). Result: Applicants emphasize **contribution**, not CV length.

**Intervention 2: Preprint Culture Normalization**

- **Current system:** Journals gatekeep dissemination; unpublished work invisible.
- **Problem:** Authors desperate to publish **anywhere** to gain visibility.
- **Solution:** Normalize **preprint servers** (SSRN, arXiv, bioRxiv) as primary dissemination. Journals become **post-hoc certification**, not gatekeeping. Reduces publication pressure.

**Example:** Physics community: arXiv dominates dissemination; journal publication secondary.

**Intervention 3: Open Peer Review with Reviewer Credit**

- **Current system:** Anonymous review; reviewers unpaid, uncredited.
- **Problem:** Low-quality review (no accountability); PSO journals fake review (no detection).
- **Solution:** **Public peer review** (reviews published alongside papers). Reviewers receive **credit** (DOI for reviews, counts toward evaluation). Incentivizes quality, exposes fake review.

**Example:** eLife (journal) publishes reviews publicly; F1000Research allows post-publication review.

**Intervention 4: Funding Agency Requirements**

- **Current system:** Grants require X publications in "peer-reviewed journals" (undefined).
- **Problem:** PSO journals meet criteria (claim peer review, provide DOI).
- **Solution:** Funding agencies require **specific indexing** (Scopus, Web of Science, PubMed) or **explicit journal whitelists**. Excludes PSO by default.

**Example:** European Research Council (ERC) de-emphasizes journal impact factors, requires narrative research descriptions.

**Intervention 5: Author Education (Necessary but Insufficient)**

- **Current approach:** Teach researchers to recognize red flags (Think.Check.Submit campaign).
- **Problem:** High verification costs remain; desperate authors submit anyway.
- **Complementary role:** Education **plus** incentive modification. Alone, education insufficient.

**PSO Index contribution:** Provides **real-time diagnostic tool** for author education. Researchers can apply index to any invitation in <5 minutes.

### 5.3.3 Why Individual Responsibility Narratives Fail

**Common rhetoric:** "Authors who publish in predatory journals are complicit; they damage science."

**EPT+PSO critique:** This narrative misidentifies the problem.

1. **Selection pressure explanation:** Authors face "publish or perish." PSO journals exploit this pressure. Blaming authors is like blaming cuckoo host birds for "choosing" to provision cuckoo chicks. The behavior is **not voluntary** but **selected**.

2. **Information asymmetry:** Many authors (especially early-career, non-Western, non-native English speakers) lack expertise to distinguish predatory from legitimate journals. Blaming them for exploitation is victim-blaming.

3. **Systemic dysfunction:** The **root cause** is institutional reliance on publication counts. Authors respond to incentives. Changing behavior requires changing incentives.

**Implication:** Policy must focus on **institutions** (universities, funding agencies, hiring committees), not **individuals** (authors).

## 5.4 Generalization: PSO Framework Beyond Academic Publishing

The EPT+PSO framework applies to **any domain** where:

1. **Credential tokens** substitute for direct quality evaluation.
2. **Mimicry costs** are lower than genuine production costs.
3. **Verification costs** are high for participants.
4. **Selection pressures** reward volume over quality.

### 5.4.1 Citation Rings: Artificial Impact Inflation

**Phenomenon:** Authors collude to cite each other's papers without genuine intellectual engagement, inflating citation counts and journal impact factors.

**PSO mechanism:**

- **Replicator:** Citation count (metric token signaling influence).
- **Phenotype:** Collaborative citation networks (mimics genuine intellectual community).
- **Function claimed:** Citations reflect intellectual value.
- **Function actual:** Citations reflect coordination, not value.
- **Stability:** As long as institutions reward citation counts, citation rings persist.

**Evidence:** Systematic citation cartels documented in economics (Frey 2003), management (Starbuck 2005), and interdisciplinary journals (Fister et al. 2016).

**EPT+PSO prediction:** Citation ring detection (e.g., algorithmic identification of reciprocal citation clusters) triggers **mimicry improvement** (more sophisticated networks, cross-discipline coordination). Arms race, not solution.

### 5.4.2 Conference Mills: Pay-to-Present Credential Vending

**Phenomenon:** Conferences accept all submissions (conditional on registration fee), provide "presented at conference" credential without peer review.

**PSO mechanism:**

- **Replicator:** Conference presentation token (signals dissemination).
- **Phenotype:** Conference infrastructure (mimics selective academic gatherings).
- **Function claimed:** Presentations selected by quality.
- **Function actual:** Presentations selected by payment.
- **Stability:** As long as CVs reward conference presentations, mills persist.

**Example:** Thousands of "international conferences" held annually in tourist destinations, accepting papers in any field, charging USD 300–1,000 registration fees.

**EPT+PSO prediction:** Blacklisting conferences (like predatory journals) fails; new conferences replace blacklisted ones. Solution: Institutions stop rewarding conference presentations without context (invited keynote vs. pay-to-present).

### 5.4.3 Ranking Manipulation: Journal Impact Factor Gaming

**Phenomenon:** Journals manipulate impact factors through coercive citation (requiring authors to cite journal papers as condition of acceptance), self-citation cartels, and editorial manipulation.

**PSO mechanism:**

- **Replicator:** Journal impact factor (metric token signaling prestige).
- **Phenotype:** Citation practices (mimics genuine intellectual influence).
- **Function claimed:** Impact factor reflects journal quality.
- **Function actual:** Impact factor reflects gaming strategy.
- **Stability:** As long as hiring/promotion committees use impact factors, gaming persists.

**Evidence:** Multiple journals **caught** manipulating impact factors (e.g., *Revista Médica de Chile* in 2007, *Gene* in 2011), yet practice persists.

**EPT+PSO prediction:** Detection triggers better gaming strategies (more subtle coercive citation, cross-journal cartels). San Francisco Declaration on Research Assessment (DORA, 2012) calls for abandoning impact factors; adoption remains incomplete.

### 5.4.4 Diploma Mills: Credential Vending Without Education

**Phenomenon:** Institutions grant degrees without education (correspondence courses, purchased diplomas, "life experience" credit).

**PSO mechanism:**

- **Replicator:** Academic degree (credential token signaling expertise).
- **Phenotype:** University infrastructure (mimics educational institutions).
- **Function claimed:** Degrees certify knowledge acquisition.
- **Function actual:** Degrees certify payment.
- **Stability:** As long as employers accept degrees without verification, mills persist.

**Evidence:** US Federal Trade Commission (FTC) shut down multiple diploma mills (e.g., Kennedy-Western University, Trinity Southern University), yet new mills emerge.

**EPT+PSO prediction:** Regulatory action eliminates individual mills but not the niche. Solution: Employers require **accreditation verification** (regional accreditation, not national or international) and **competency testing** (not just degree possession).

### 5.4.5 Certification Agencies: Compliance Theater

**Phenomenon:** Third-party certifiers (ISO standards, industry certifications) provide compliance credentials without genuine auditing.

**PSO mechanism:**

- **Replicator:** Certification credential (signals regulatory compliance).
- **Phenotype:** Auditing infrastructure (mimics genuine oversight).
- **Function claimed:** Certification validates compliance.
- **Function actual:** Certification validates payment.
- **Stability:** As long as regulations accept certification without independent verification, agencies persist.

**Evidence:** ISO certification scandals (e.g., companies certified despite non-compliance), auditing firms colluding with clients (e.g., Enron-Arthur Andersen).

**EPT+PSO prediction:** Regulatory tightening triggers mimicry improvement (better auditing documentation, more sophisticated collusion). Solution: **Liability regimes** holding certifiers accountable for failures.

## 5.5 Theoretical Robustness: Responding to Ten Objections

This section summarizes responses to ten objections (full details in Appendix B):

### 5.5.1 Objection 1: "It's Just Fraud, Not Spontaneous Order"

**Response:** PSO differs from fraud in four ways:

1. **Legality:** PSO journals often operate legally (obtain ISSN, claim undefined "peer review").
2. **Decentralization:** No mastermind; thousands of independent entities.
3. **Stability:** Fraud exposed should collapse; PSO exposed adapts (mimicry improves).
4. **Selection mechanism:** PSO emerges through competitive advantage, not criminal intent.

**Verdict:** PSO includes fraud but is broader (legal exploitation of dysfunctional incentives).

### 5.5.2 Objection 2: "This Is Just Principal-Agent Problem"

**Response:** Principal-agent requires bilateral relationship (principal hires agent, monitors, punishes shirking). PSO is **triadic** (author-journal-institution), **emergent** (no hiring), and **selection-based** (not contractual).

**Verdict:** Principal-agent is necessary but insufficient (captures author incentives, not emergence or mimicry).

### 5.5.3 Objection 3: "You're Just Being Cynical About New Journals"

**Response:** Case #1 (JCLLT) disproves this. New journal scored **2.0** (legitimate), while older journals scored **5.0** (PSO). Framework distinguishes based on **functional integrity**, not age.

**Verdict:** PSO Index has **zero false positives** in corpus.

### 5.5.4 Objection 4: "Maybe They're Just Bad at Marketing"

**Response:** Incompetence predicts **random errors**, **learning over time**, **correction when notified**. PSO predicts **systematic patterns**, **persistence despite exposure**, **no correction** (not in their interest).

**Evidence:** Patterns consistent across cases; same errors repeated; Diago case confirms stability.

**Verdict:** "Incompetence" doesn't explain stability.

### 5.5.5 Objection 5: "This Framework Applies to Legitimate Journals Too"

**Response:** Key criterion is **functional divergence**. Legitimate journals: claimed function = actual function (peer review validates quality). PSO journals: claimed function ≠ actual function (peer review absent, credential vending occurs).

**Test:** Diago experiment. Legitimate journals reject absurd papers; PSO journals accept.

**Verdict:** Framework identifies **divergence**, not all value extraction.

### 5.5.6 Objection 6: "Authors Are Complicit, Not Victims"

**Response:** EPT explains complicity without requiring awareness. Authors face selection pressure (publish or perish); PSO exploits this. "Choice" is constrained by system incentives.

**Analogy:** Cuckoo host birds don't "choose" to provision cuckoo chicks; behavior selected.

**Evidence:** Mix of exploitation (ignorant authors) and desperation (knowledgeable authors under extreme pressure).

**Verdict:** Blaming authors is victim-blaming; root cause is systemic.

### 5.5.7 Objection 7: "Regulatory Capture Explains This Better"

**Response:** Regulatory capture requires (a) regulator, (b) capture mechanism (lobbying). PSO has neither. Academic publishing is decentralized; PSO emerged **spontaneously**, not through capture.

**Verdict:** Wrong mechanism (capture vs. evolution).

### 5.5.8 Objection 8: "This Is Just a Metaphor, Not Science"

**Response:** EPT+PSO is **mechanistic**, not metaphorical. It provides:

1. **Replicator identification:** "Published paper" token.
2. **Selection pressure specification:** Metric-driven incentives.
3. **Predictions:** Mimicry improvement, content irrelevance, mismatch stability.
4. **Falsifiability:** Could be wrong (e.g., if Diago paper rejected, framework falsified).

**Diago case:** Key test. EPT+PSO predicts content irrelevant; tested; confirmed.

**Verdict:** Framework is falsifiable, predictive, mechanistic. Not metaphor.

### 5.5.9 Objection 9: "Solutions Are Obvious: Just Ban Them"

**Response:** Bans fail because:

1. **Whac-a-Mole:** New journals replace blacklisted ones faster than lists update.
2. **Lag:** Time between emergence and blacklisting allows operation.
3. **Legal:** No basis to "ban" (PSO often legal).
4. **Mimicry improvement:** Detection triggers better mimicry, not withdrawal.

**Evidence:** Beall's List shutdown 2017; predatory publishing grew afterward.

**EPT+PSO solution:** Change underlying incentives (what institutions reward), not symptoms (individual journals).

**Verdict:** Bans treat symptoms, not causes.

### 5.5.10 Objection 10: "Why Use Evolution If You're Talking About Culture?"

**Response:** Cultural evolution is real evolution. Dawkins coined **"meme"** (cultural replicator); Extended Phenotype applies to cultural phenomena.

**Requirements for evolution:**

1. **Variation:** Different journals exist (check).
2. **Selection:** Some more successful (check: PSO outcompetes high-quality in volume).
3. **Inheritance:** Successful models copied (check: new PSO entities mimic successful predecessors).

**Verdict:** Evolution is the **correct framework** for cultural phenomena exhibiting variation, selection, and inheritance.
