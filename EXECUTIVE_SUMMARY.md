## 🧬 Theoretical Framework: EPT + Parasitic Spontaneous Order

### Core Thesis
Predatory journals are not isolated fraud but **evolutionarily stable strategies** within metric-driven academic systems. They function as **extended phenotypes** of dysfunctional incentive structures, replicating institutional signals (ISSN, peer review claims) while extracting resources without epistemic contribution.

### Three Falsifiable Predictions (All Validated)
1. **Mimetic fidelity increases with exposure** → Arms race: detection improves, mimicry improves
2. **Content quality is irrelevant** → Diago case confirms: absurd papers accepted
3. **Disciplinary mismatch is stable** → 85.7% of PSO cases exhibit dramatic mismatch

### Why PSO Persists
- **Selection niche:** Publish-or-perish pressure + metric substitution + high verification costs
- **Replicator:** "Published paper" token valued independently of content
- **Stability mechanism:** Evolutionarily Stable Strategy (ESS) - persists because underlying incentives unchanged
- **Mimicry dynamics:** Blacklists drive better mimicry (ISSN acquisition, professional websites), not elimination

## 💡 Policy Implications: Incentive Modification Over Symptom Treatment

### Why Standard Approaches Fail

**❌ Blacklists (Beall's List):** Whac-a-Mole problem. New PSO journals replace blacklisted ones faster than lists update. Mimicry improves, detection arms race perpetuates.

**❌ Author education (Think.Check.Submit):** Necessary but insufficient. Verification costs remain high, desperate authors submit anyway.

**❌ Legal action:** Most PSO journals operate legally (obtain ISSN, claim undefined "peer review"). Jurisdictional difficulties with offshore entities.

### Effective Systemic Solutions

**✅ Qualitative evaluation in hiring/promotion:** Replace publication counts with narrative assessment of research contribution. Committees read papers, assess methodology. Eliminates metric gaming.

**✅ Preprint culture normalization:** Shift dissemination to preprint servers (SSRN, arXiv); journals become post-hoc certification, not gatekeeping. Reduces publication pressure.

**✅ Open peer review + reviewer credit:** Publish reviews alongside papers; credit reviewers with DOI. Incentivizes quality review, exposes fake review.

**✅ Funding agency requirements:** Require specific indexing (Scopus, Web of Science) or narrative research descriptions (not CV length). Excludes PSO by design.

**Key insight:** PSO persists because institutions reward **publication volume** (easily gamed) over **research quality** (costly to evaluate). Must change selection environment, not eliminate individual journals.

## 🔄 Generalizability: Five Domain Extensions

The EPT+PSO framework applies to **any domain** where:
1. Credential tokens substitute for direct quality evaluation
2. Mimicry costs < genuine production costs
3. Verification costs are high
4. Selection pressures reward volume over quality

### Extensions Outlined (Section 5.4)

1. **Citation rings:** Authors collude to inflate citation counts without genuine engagement
2. **Conference mills:** Pay-to-present events provide "conference presentation" credential without peer review
3. **Ranking manipulation:** Journals game impact factors through coercive citation, self-citation cartels
4. **Diploma mills:** Institutions grant degrees without education (purchased diplomas, "life experience" credit)
5. **Certification agencies:** Third-party certifiers provide compliance credentials without genuine auditing

**Theoretical unification:** All exhibit same mechanism—replicators (credential tokens) proliferate through mimicry (extended phenotypes) under selection pressures (metric-driven evaluation).
