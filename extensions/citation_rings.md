# Extension: Citation Rings as PSO

**Status:** Proposed extension - empirical validation pending

---

## Phenomenon

**Citation rings:** Networks of papers that cite each other primarily to inflate metrics (h-index, impact factor) without genuine intellectual engagement.

---

## PSO Markers Analysis

### 1. Mimics Legitimate Function

**Legitimate citations:**
- Indicate intellectual debt
- Guide readers to related work
- Build cumulative knowledge

**Citation ring citations:**
- **Claimed function:** Same as above
- **Actual function:** Metric manipulation
- **Divergence:** Complete

### 2. Emergent, Not Coordinated (Usually)

**Two types:**

**Type A: Explicit conspiracy**
- Authors agree to cite each other
- Detected by graph analysis (dense cliques)
- **Not PSO** (coordinated, not emergent)

**Type B: Implicit convergence**
- Authors independently cite papers that cite them
- "You cited me, so I cite you" norm
- Emerges without agreement
- **IS PSO** (spontaneous reciprocity)

### 3. Extractive Without Contribution

**Extracts:**
- Inflated h-indices
- Journal impact factors
- Hiring/promotion benefits

**Contributes:**
- Nothing (citations don't indicate quality)

---

## Extended Phenotype Interpretation

### Replicator

**Unit:** Citation as recognition token

**Selection pressure:** Metric-driven evaluation systems

### Phenotype

**Structure:** Citation network (graph structure)

**Function (claimed):** Knowledge map

**Function (actual):** Metric inflation map

### Selection Environment

**Pressure:**
- Hiring committees check h-index
- Journals advertise impact factor
- Funding agencies use citation metrics

**Result:** Selection for citation accumulation, not quality

---

## Empirical Predictions

**If citation rings are PSO, we predict:**

1. **Clustering:** Citations within ring denser than to outside
2. **Reciprocity:** Higher mutual citation rates than expected
3. **Content irrelevance:** Citation text doesn't engage cited work
4. **Stability:** Persists despite exposure
5. **Evolution:** More sophisticated hiding as detection improves

---

## Detection Methods

### Graph Analysis

**Metrics:**
1. **Clustering coefficient:** Measure of local density
2. **Reciprocity index:** Mutual vs. one-way citations
3. **Content analysis:** Semantic relevance of citation context

### Red Flags

- Cliques with >80% internal citations
- Papers citing each other within weeks of publication
- Generic citation language ("as shown in [X]")
- Same author sets across multiple papers

**Scenario:**

**PSO Score (proposed):**
- Clustering: ✅
- Reciprocity: ✅
- Low external validation: ✅
- Content irrelevance: ✅
- **Classification:** PSO confirmed

## Relationship to Predatory Journals

**Synergistic PSO:**

Predatory journals + citation rings = **amplified parasitism**

**Mechanism:**
1. Author pays APC to predatory journal
2. Paper published with DOI
3. Author cites own papers in other submissions
4. Citation ring forms naturally
5. Metrics inflate
6. No quality validation at any step

**Result:** Double parasitism (PSO × PSO)

---

## Why This Matters

**If citation rings are PSO:**
- Can't be solved by policing alone
- Need to change underlying incentives
- Same framework (EPT) applies
- Generalizes beyond publishing

**If not PSO:**
- Different solution set needed
- Maybe principal-agent problem instead
- Maybe fraud (explicit coordination)

## Empirical Validation Plan

1. **Data collection:** Download citation network from Web of Science/Scopus
2. **Identify suspected rings:** High reciprocity clusters
3. **Test PSO criteria:**
   - Check if emergent vs. coordinated
   - Measure functional divergence
   - Assess mimicry quality
4. **Compare to control:** Legitimate citation networks in same field
5. **Validate predictions:** Does exposure lead to mimicry improvement?

---

## Connection to Academic PSO

**Shared features:**
- Metric exploitation
- Mimetic legitimacy
- Functional divergence
- Stability despite exposure

**Differences:**
- Citation rings exploit existing infrastructure
- Predatory journals create new infrastructure
- But both are **parasitic spontaneous orders**

---

## Open Questions

1. **Emergence threshold:** How many reciprocal cites before it's a "ring"?
2. **Intent:** Can PSO apply even if authors don't intend manipulation?
3. **Field variation:** Are some fields more vulnerable?
4. **Journal role:** Do predatory journals enable rings more than legitimate?

---

## Policy Implications

**If validated as PSO:**

**Don't do:**
- Manual policing of citation networks (whac-a-mole)
- Blacklisting specific authors (new ones emerge)

**Do:**
- Reduce weight of citation metrics in evaluation
- Use qualitative assessment of contribution
- Implement graph-analysis detection at system level
- Change incentives (reward quality, not quantity)

---

## Conclusion

Citation rings **plausibly** fit PSO framework:
- Emergent (at least Type B)
- Mimetic (look like legitimate citations)
- Extractive (inflate metrics without contribution)
- Stable (persist despite exposure)

**Empirical validation needed** before definitive classification.

**If validated:** Strengthens PSO as general framework beyond publishing venues.
