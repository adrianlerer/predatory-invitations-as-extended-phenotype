# Stress Test Cases: Objections to PSO Framework

This document addresses potential objections to the Parasitic Spontaneous Order (PSO) framework through theoretical stress testing.

---

## Objection 1: "It's Just Fraud, Not Spontaneous Order"

**Claim:** Predatory journals are criminal fraud, not emergent order

**Response:**

**Fraud requires:** Intent to deceive + material false representation + victim reliance + damages

**PSO exhibits:**
- Decentralized emergence (no mastermind)
- Legally defensible claims (have ISSN, provide DOI)
- Operates WITHIN system rules (not illegal)
- Victims could verify but don't (costly information)

**Key difference:**
- **Fraud:** Violation of law
- **PSO:** Exploitation of legal but dysfunctional incentives

**Example:** Legally obtaining ISSN, claiming "peer review" (undefined term), charging APC - all LEGAL

**Conclusion:** PSO ≠ fraud (though may be unethical)

---

## Objection 2: "This Is Just Principal-Agent Problem"

**Claim:** Standard principal-agent theory explains this

**Response:**

**Principal-Agent requires:**
- Identifiable principal and agent
- Contract relationship
- Monitoring difficulty

**PSO differs:**
- **No bilateral relationship** - triadic (author-journal-institution)
- **Emergent structure** - no one "hired" PSO
- **Selection mechanism** - evolutionary, not contractual

**Principal-Agent can't explain:**
- Why PSO emerges without being hired
- Why it mimics institutional forms
- Why it's stable despite exposure

**EPT adds:** Gene's-eye view - asks "what replicates?" not "who's the agent?"

**Conclusion:** Principal-agent is **necessary but insufficient**

## Objection 3: "You're Just Being Cynical About New Journals"

**Claim:** Framework rejects all emerging journals unfairly

**Response:**

**Case #1 (JCLLT) proves otherwise:**
- New journal (no prior volumes)
- Passed all PSO tests
- Accepted by author
- **PSO Score: 0/5**

**Distinguishing features:**
- Verifiable editor (LinkedIn, credentials)
- Field alignment perfect
- Professional infrastructure (OJS)
- No aggressive follow-ups
- Transparent policies

**PSO framework has ZERO false positives in corpus (1/8 legitimate correctly identified)**

**Conclusion:** Framework distinguishes legitimate emerging journals from PSO

## Objection 4: "Maybe They're Just Bad at Marketing"

**Claim:** Template errors, mismatches are incompetence, not parasitism

**Response:**

**Incompetence hypothesis predicts:**
- Random errors
- Learning over time
- Correction when notified

**PSO hypothesis predicts:**
- Systematic patterns
- Persistence despite exposure
- No correction (not in their interest)

**Evidence supports PSO:**
- Patterns consistent across cases
- Same errors repeated (Case #2: multiple emails with same template error)
- No improvement despite public exposure (Diago case still accepting absurd papers)

**Functional divergence is STABLE:**
- Because it's adaptive
- Not accidental
- Selected FOR, not against

**Conclusion:** "Incompetence" doesn't explain stability

## Objection 5: "This Framework Applies to Legitimate Journals Too"

**Claim:** All journals extract value, so framework is too broad

**Response:**

**Key criterion: Functional divergence**

**Legitimate journals:**
- **Claimed function:** Validate quality through peer review
- **Actual function:** Validate quality through peer review
- **Alignment:** HIGH

**PSO journals:**
- **Claimed function:** Validate quality
- **Actual function:** Credential vending
- **Divergence:** COMPLETE

**Test:** Diago experiment
- Absurd paper submitted
- Legitimate journals: REJECT
- PSO journals: ACCEPT (for fee)

**Conclusion:** Framework identifies **divergence**, not all value extraction

## Objection 6: "Authors Are Complicit, Not Victims"

**Claim:** Authors know it's predatory but submit anyway

**Response:**

**Evidence against:**
- Template errors surprise authors
- Field mismatches noticed
- Authors ask for verification help

**EPT explains complicity without requiring awareness:**
- Authors face selection pressure (publish or perish)
- PSO exploits this pressure
- Author "choice" is constrained by system incentives

**Analogy:** Cuckoo host birds don't "choose" to raise cuckoo chicks

**Some authors know, most don't:**
- Corpus author (sophisticated) rejected 7/8
- But 87.5% PSO rate suggests many authors DON'T reject

**Conclusion:** Mix of exploitation (ignorance) and desperation (knowledge)

## Objection 7: "Regulatory Capture Explains This Better"

**Claim:** Regulatory capture framework is more parsimonious

**Response:**

**Regulatory capture requires:**
- Regulator (who?)
- Regulated industry
- Capture mechanism (lobbying, revolving door)

**PSO context:**
- **No regulator** - academic publishing is decentralized
- **No capture** - no one was "captured"
- **Spontaneous** - no lobbying occurred

**What emerged:**
- Without regulation
- Without coordination
- Through selection on existing incentives

**EPT explains emergence; regulatory capture doesn't**

**Conclusion:** Wrong mechanism (capture vs. evolution)

## Objection 8: "This Is Just a Metaphor, Not Science"

**Claim:** Extended phenotype is metaphorical application

**Response:**

**EPT as metaphor:**
- Loose analogy
- Not predictive
- Not falsifiable

**EPT as mechanism:**
- **Replicator identified:** "Published paper" token
- **Selection pressure specified:** Metric-driven incentives
- **Predictions generated:** Mimicry, stability, diversification
- **Falsifiable:** Could be wrong

**This paper provides:**
- Operational definition (PSO Index)
- Testable predictions
- Empirical validation (Diago case)

**Diago case: KEY TEST**
- EPT predicts: Content irrelevant
- Tested: Absurd paper submitted

## Objection 9: "Solutions Are Obvious - Just Ban Them"

**Claim:** Lists like Beall's solve the problem

**Response:**

**Why bans don't work:**

1. **Whac-a-Mole:** New journals emerge constantly
2. **Lag:** Time between emergence and identification
3. **Legal:** No basis to "ban" (not illegal)
4. **Evolution:** PSO adapts (better mimicry)

**Evidence:**
- Beall's List stopped updating (2017)
- Predatory publishing grew AFTER exposure
- New tactics emerged (better mimicry)

**EPT predicts:**
- Selection pressure remains → PSO persists
- Detection → mimicry improves
- Arms race, not solution

**Real solution:**
- Change underlying incentives
- Not blacklists

**Conclusion:** Bans treat symptoms, not causes

## Objection 10: "Why Use Evolution If You're Talking About Culture?"

**Claim:** Cultural transmission ≠ genetic evolution

**Response:**

**Agreed - but:**

**Cultural evolution is real evolution IF:**
1. **Variation:** Different journals exist
2. **Selection:** Some more successful
3. **Inheritance:** Successful models copied

**All three present in PSO:**
1. **Variation:** Different mimicry strategies
2. **Selection:** Volume strategy outcompetes quality
3. **Inheritance:** Successful PSO copied by new entrants

**Dawkins himself:**
- Coined "meme" (cultural replicator)
- Extended phenotype applies to cultural evolution
- Selection operates on ANY replicator

**This paper uses:** Memetic framework (cultural evolution)

**Conclusion:** Evolution is the RIGHT framework for cultural phenomena

## Refined Framework After Stress Testing

**PSO is:**
1. **Emergent** (not planned)
2. **Legal** (not criminal)
3. **Selective** (distinguishes legitimate from parasitic)
4. **Mechanistic** (not metaphorical)
5. **Predictive** (generates testable hypotheses)
6. **Cultural-evolutionary** (appropriate framework)
7. **Stable** (not accidental)
8. **Systemic** (not individual)

**PSO is NOT:**
1. Fraud (though unethical)
2. Simple principal-agent problem
3. Blanket rejection of new journals
4. Mere incompetence
5. Universal to all publishing
6. Regulatory capture
7. Metaphor only
8. Solvable by bans alone
